General Instructions & Typing Basics

PLEASE READ CAREFULLY
Mandatory: Use a Physical Keyboard Only
To build actual muscle memory required for government exams, do not use a mobile touchscreen or on-screen keyboard. Connect a physical USB or Bluetooth keyboard.

1. The Home Row (Finger Placement)

Before you begin, place your fingers exactly on the Home Row. This is your anchor point:

Left Hand:
  • Pinky finger on A
  • Ring finger on S
  • Middle finger on D
  • Index finger on F
Right Hand:
  • Index finger on J
  • Middle finger on K
  • Ring finger on L
  • Pinky finger on ; (semicolon)

Tip: You will feel small physical bumps on the F and J keys. Use these to find the home row without looking down. Rest both thumbs lightly on the Spacebar.

2. Typing Technique & Posture

  • Do not look at the keyboard. Keep your eyes glued to the screen text at all times.
  • Sit up straight with your feet flat on the floor. Keep the keyboard at belly-button height.
  • Keep your wrists slightly elevated above the keyboard; do not rest them heavily on the desk.
  • Strike the keys with quick, light, snappy taps. Return your fingers to the home keys immediately.
  • Use the Shift key on the opposite side of the letter you are typing to capitalize.

3. Official Exam Scoring Rules & Goals

  • Calculation Standard: 1 Word = Exactly 5 Keystrokes.
  • Gross WPM: (Total keystrokes / 5) / Time in minutes.
  • Net WPM: ((Total keystrokes - Error Keystrokes) / 5) / Time in minutes.
  • Spaces count as keystrokes. Skipped letters, wrong letters, or extra spaces count as errors. Excessive backspaces reduce your gross speed.
  • Target Goals for Qualification:
    • SSC CHSL: 35 WPM, 95% Accuracy
    • SSC CGL Tier-II: 30 WPM, 95% Accuracy
    • Bank PO: 40 WPM, 95% Accuracy
